Oil storage dynamics at Kharg export terminal

Data and methodology

Kharg island, Iran, hosts the countryâ€™s primary crude oil export terminal. A significant share of Iranian seaborne exports departs from this location. Independent storage estimates at major export hubs provide additional insight into supply dynamics and inventory shifts.

The chart represents a weekly estimate of total crude oil stored across the main above-ground storage tanks located at the Kharg export complex. The calculation aggregates all identified large cylindrical tanks within the facility.

How the estimate is produced

The model uses Sentinel-1 C-band synthetic aperture radar imagery. Radar backscatter from floating-roof tanks varies depending on roof position: when the tank is full, the metallic roof sits higher; when empty, the roof drops, altering radar reflection geometry. These variations are used to derive a relative fill ratio for each tank.

Tank diameters are mapped individually. Assuming cylindrical geometry, estimated fill ratios are converted into volumetric values and then expressed in barrels. Weekly values are calculated by aggregating observations and smoothing short-term noise.

Model assumptions

The figures represent a modeled estimate derived from radar signal behavior. The series should be interpreted primarily as a directional storage trend indicator rather than an exact statement of absolute inventory. Synthetic aperture radar enables consistent observation regardless of daylight or weather conditions.
